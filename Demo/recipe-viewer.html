Cooking time: 1 h 50 min

Difficulty: ★★★☆☆

Ingredients:

1 pork loin (about 3-4 pounds)

4 cloves of garlic, minced

2 tablespoons of olive oil

2 teaspoons of fresh thyme, chopped

2 teaspoons of fresh rosemary, chopped

1 teaspoon of fresh sage, chopped

Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

2. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, olive oil, chopped thyme, rosemary, and sage to create a herb-garlic paste.

3. Pat the pork loin dry with paper towels and place it on a cutting board. Using a sharp knife, make several shallow cuts all over the surface of the pork loin.

4. Rub the herb-garlic paste all over the pork loin, making sure to massage it into the cuts you made.

5. Season the pork loin generously with salt and pepper on all sides.

6. Place the pork loin in a roasting pan or a baking dish, and insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the loin, making sure it doesn't touch the bone if your cut has one.

7. Roast the pork loin in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare, or up to 160°F (71°C) for well done. Make sure to baste the pork loin with the juices from the pan every 20-30 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful.

8. Once the pork loin is cooked to your desired doneness, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 10-15 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.

9. Slice the roasted pork loin into thick slices and serve hot with your favorite side dishes, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad.

Enjoy your delicious roasted pork loin with herbs and garlic!
